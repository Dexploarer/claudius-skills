### 2. MoSCoW Method
- **M** - Must have (critical, non-negotiable)
- **S** - Should have (important but not vital)
- **C** - Could have (nice to have if time permits)
- **W** - Won't have (not priority right now)

### 3. Value vs. Effort Matrix
```
High Value, Low Effort   ‚Üí DO THESE FIRST (quick wins)
High Value, High Effort  ‚Üí PLAN & SCHEDULE (strategic projects)
Low Value, Low Effort    ‚Üí BATCH OR DELEGATE (if time allows)
Low Value, High Effort   ‚Üí AVOID (not worth it)
```

### 4. ICE Scoring (Impact, Confidence, Ease)
Rate each task 1-10 on:
- **Impact**: How much does this move the needle?
- **Confidence**: How sure are you about the impact?
- **Ease**: How easy is this to implement?

**Score = (Impact √ó Confidence √ó Ease) / 100**

Higher scores = higher priority

### 5. Warren Buffett's 25/5 Rule
1. List 25 tasks/goals
2. Circle your top 5
3. **Avoid the other 20 at all costs** (they distract from what matters)

## Process:

1. **List all tasks** the user needs to prioritize
2. **Ask clarifying questions:**
   - What's the deadline?
   - What's the impact if this doesn't get done?
   - How long will this take?
   - Who else is waiting on this?
   - Does this align with your goals?

3. **Apply most relevant framework** (or multiple)
4. **Output prioritized list** with reasoning
5. **Suggest action plan** (what to do now, next, later)
