# Brainstorm Facilitator

**Trigger phrases:**
- "brainstorm ideas for"
- "help me ideate"
- "creative ideas about"
- "brainstorming session"
- "generate ideas for"
- "what are some ideas for"

**Description:**
Facilitates structured brainstorming sessions using proven creative thinking techniques. Helps generate, organize, and refine ideas for any challenge or opportunity.

## What this skill does:

When you provide a challenge, goal, or opportunity, this skill will:

1. **Apply proven brainstorming techniques:**
   - Mind mapping
   - SCAMPER method (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse)
   - Six Thinking Hats
   - Reverse brainstorming
   - Rapid ideation

2. **Generate diverse ideas:**
   - Conventional and unconventional approaches
   - Quick wins and ambitious innovations
   - Low-cost and high-investment options
   - Short-term and long-term solutions

3. **Organize and categorize:**
   - Group similar ideas
   - Identify themes and patterns
   - Highlight most promising concepts
   - Note dependencies and prerequisites

4. **Refine and prioritize:**
   - Evaluate feasibility
   - Assess impact/effort ratio
   - Suggest next steps
   - Create action-ready concepts

## SCAMPER Analysis

### Substitute
- Replace email notifications with in-app notifications
- Substitute static content with interactive content

### Combine
- Combine shopping with social (shop + share)
- Merge loyalty program with gamification

### Adapt
- Adapt successful features from other apps (Duolingo's streaks, Instagram's stories)
- Borrow game mechanics from popular games

### Modify
- Modify onboarding to be more engaging (interactive tutorial)
- Enhance push notifications with rich media

### Put to other uses
- Use app for customer support (not just sales)
- Enable app for community building

### Eliminate
- Remove unnecessary steps in user journey
- Eliminate annoying pop-ups/interruptions

### Reverse
- Instead of pushing content, let users pull (customizable feed)
- Reward for NOT doing something (digital wellness)

## The "3-Contact Rule" Strategy

Research shows friendships form through repeated, unplanned interactions:

1. **First contact:** Meetup/event (say hello, be friendly)
2. **Second contact:** Same group/place (deeper conversation)
3. **Third contact:** Suggest 1-on-1 ("Want to grab coffee?")

**Key:** You must make the ask. Don't wait for them.

## Brainstorming techniques used:

1. **Mind Mapping** - Organized ideas by category
2. **SCAMPER** - Systematic creative thinking
3. **Reverse Brainstorming** - Identify what NOT to do
4. **Priority Matrix** - Impact vs. Effort analysis
5. **Rapid Ideation** - Quantity first, quality later
6. **Six Thinking Hats** - Multiple perspectives
7. **Action Planning** - From ideas to execution

## Best Practices:

1. **Quantity over quality (at first):**
   - Generate lots of ideas
   - Don't judge during brainstorming
   - Refine later

2. **Be specific:**
   - Vague: "Market better"
   - Better: "Create Instagram Reels showing product use cases"

3. **Consider constraints:**
   - Mention budget, timeline, resources
   - Generates more realistic ideas

4. **Mix conventional and wild:**
   - Some safe bets
   - Some moonshots
